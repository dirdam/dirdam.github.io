Maths apps

  • Bounded functions:
    Visualize how an ℝ2 function would look like if the plane was to be bounded in a [-1,1] × [-1,1] square.
  • Phyllotaxis:
    Phyllotaxis is the arrangement of leaves on a plant stem. See a sunflower-like pattern grow!
  • Predator-Prey:
    Imagine: If the number of lynxes (predator) grow in an area, the number of hares (prey) will decrease. If the hares decrease, less lynxes will have food, therefore they too will decrease in number. If the number of lynxes decrease, the hares will proliferate faster since there are less predators. This will lead to a bigger number of hares, which will stimulate the growth of lynxes too, what takes us to the first step again. Formally, this problem is modelized by the Lotka-Volterra equations.
  • Tree representation:
    Draw a tree (or forest) graph and obtain its relative area, volume and height based on its cuboidal representation.

Games

  • Azi:
    The apple of my eye, Azi is a game I came up with during a stay in China, March 2015. The game confronts two players on a square grid. The standard game is played on an 8×8 grid, although it can be played on any arbitrary size. See rules.
    • Pieces only move vertically (in their own column).
    • Pieces advance as much as the value under their starting cell says.
    • When a piece reaches the last row, the player in turn says "Fratama!" and turns over the piece (so that it shows that it is going back). The new value for the piece to advance is the one written on top of the row. The current direction of advance is always where the triangle points.
    • If a player performs a move and there is an opponent's piece in the way:
      1. 1. The player in turn says "Spar!".
      2. 2. The player's piece moves to one cell further than where the opponent's piece was.
      3. 3. The opponent's piece moves back to its last staring row (the piece conserves its orientation; there is no turn over).
      4. 4. If there is yet another opponent's piece in the new cell, repeat from step 1.
    • When a piece gets back to the row where it started the game, the player in turn says "Bitya!" and the piece ends its game. The piece should be left in the row without being turned over.
    • When the last piece of a player reaches the starting row, the player in turn says "Asta!" and wins the game. The game is over.
    • During the game loops can occur (reach to the exact same position in which both players were some states ago). In this case, the player dragged into the loop should say "Hama!", and the opponent (who started the loop) should be the one to break it by moving a different piece.
  • Gyrux:
    Turn the pieces, make them rotate. The goal is to end up with an all-squares design, matching colors properly.
  • HexaWars:
    HexaWars is a strategy game I came up with in March 2014. Color conquests in hexagonal grids. Its rules' full understanding will take some practice, but the learning and playing pays back. The rules are explained here (PDF).
